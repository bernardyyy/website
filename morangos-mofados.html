Moldy Strawberries: Form and Fragmentation in a Time of Crisis

Editorial

2024

Morangos Mofados (Moldy Strawberries) is a collection of stories by celebrated Brazilian writer Caio Fernando Abreu.

The landscape onto which the original book appeared in Brazil included the AIDS epidemic, the final throes of dictatorship, an external debt crisis and economic recession, and a wide range of geopolitical and ecological anxieties.

Divided into three main parts: Strawberry, The Mold, and Moldy Strawberries.

In the book, the circles represent the mold seeping into each chapter, adopting a unique color system for each section.
